Plating Playbook

Reusable patterns for beautiful plates

Learn five simple compositions you can apply to bowls, plates, and boards. Think of them as templates—swap in your ingredients and go.

1. The Swoosh

Swipe a spoonful of sauce across the plate. Anchor with a round element (protein or veg), then scatter garnishes in odd numbers.

2. The Clock

Place components at 12, 4, and 8 o’clock. Keeps negative space open and looks balanced from any angle.

3. The Stack

Layer a base, mid, and top. Offset slightly so each layer peeks out. Finish with height—a herb sprig or crunch.

4. The Bowl Nest

Create a shallow well with grains or noodles. Arrange toppings in sections, then drizzle sauce in a zig-zag.

5. The Stripe

Lay a thick stripe of purée or yogurt. Place protein along it, then align garnish in a tidy row for rhythm.

Finishing touches
  • Wipe the rim with a damp towel between steps.
  • Add contrast: something creamy, crunchy, and bright.
  • Micro-herbs beat big leaves for elegance.
  • Use warm plates for hot food—keeps sauces glossy.
